Why should your child join ZugZwang?



Imagine this. A parent is waiting in the reception to pick up the child after class. The class is long over. A few kids are still in the class lost in the activity that has engaged them. Whether it is the silent contemplation in a chess game or the animated discussion with the teacher in robotics, the common theme - they have lost track of time. This plays out every week at ZugZwang Academy.

This was before the lockdown. In the last few months, we’ve translated this level of flow & engagement into the online world. With renewal rates of close to 90%, our programs have been a runaway hit with kids, who often skip school lectures to attend our classes. Through a variety of activities, challenges, portals such as ChessKid & FlipGrid, we’ve been able to capture the imagination of kids to learn in a fun & balanced manner.

This is your average ZugZwang child - smart, intelligent, intellectual, intrinsically motivated and in flow with supreme focus & concentration. The average child in today’s world is often driven by extrinsic factors such as fear (what would happen if you fail?) or greed (study, and I”ll buy you a gaming system). At ZugZwang, our kids are motivated intrinsically. They do things sans incentives and threats. They love what they do. But, how do we achieve that? Read on.

To motivate a child intrinsically, we tap into the positive emotions. After all, emotions are the motive force for action. And we refrain from using negative emotional drivers such as guilt, shame, fear or greed. The positive factors include

Curiosity - nothing like invoking a child’s natural curiosity for things. Our method of structuring all classes as challenges helps us stoke the curiosity into a fire for learning.

Natural Interest - Activities a child loves doing be it chess or Rubik’s. Natural interest is an activity or a task that has the least amount of resistance and greatest amount of action-orientation.

Overcoming Challenges - The feeling of achieving a goal and overcoming a challenge is a great driver for kids to do something. Done over a consistent period it helps build great habits, work ethic and intrinsic motivation.

Trust & Respect - If education was just content transfer, we would all be buying online apps for our children. Anyone who has tried it will vouch that it simply doesn’t work. Children learn because they create bonds with their mentors. It is a relationship of trust & respect. We ensure our mentors build that with children.

Social circle - We have hundreds of kids with similar aspirations and drives engaged in our programs. Collaboration is part of learning. Children connect with like minded peers and what flows out of this quasi- competitive and collaborative environment is the drive to continuously excel.

To achieve this all of our classes in all the programs follow this structure.
